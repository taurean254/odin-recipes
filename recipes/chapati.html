Chapatis

Description

Chapatis are a popular, unleavened flatbread widely enjoyed as a staple food in the Indian subcontinent and a cherished delicacy and comfort food in East Africa. They are known for their soft texture, simple ingredients, and versatility in pairing with a wide variety of dishes.

Ingredients

  • All-Purpose Flour (White Flour): to achieve a softer, chewier result.
  • Water: To form the dough
  • Salt: For seasoning.
  • Cooking Oil or Ghee: This is crucial. It is used in the dough, for brushing the layers during rolling, and sometimes for cooking on the griddle to achieve the flaky texture.
  • Sugar (optional): A small pinch is sometimes added for a subtle sweetness that balances the salt.

Steps

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine your all-purpose flour, salt, and optional sugar.
  2. Knead the Dough: Gradually add warm water and about 2 tablespoons of oil to the flour mixture. Knead the dough vigorously for 10-15 minutes until it is soft, smooth, elastic, and no longer sticky.
  3. Rest the Dough: Cover the dough with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour. This step is crucial for gluten development, which makes the dough pliable and the final chapatis soft.
  4. Portion the Dough: Divide the rested dough into equal, golf-ball-sized portions and roll each into a ball.
  5. Create Layers (The Spiral Method): On a lightly floured surface, roll one dough ball into a thin, large circle. Lightly brush the entire surface of the rolled-out dough with cooking oil or melted ghee. Starting from one edge, roll the oiled circle tightly into a long, rope-like log. Coil the rope into a spiral (like a snail shell) and tuck the end piece underneath to secure it. Repeat this layering process for all the dough balls.
  6. Rest Layered Dough: Cover the coiled dough balls and let them rest for another 10-15 minutes.
  7. Final Roll: Take one coiled dough ball and gently flatten it with your palm. Roll it out into a final, even circle (about 8-10 inches in diameter) using a rolling pin.
  8. Heat the Pan: Heat a non-stick frying pan or tava over medium heat. It should be hot enough to sizzle a drop of water but not so hot that it burns the chapati immediately.
  9. Cook the First Side: Place the rolled chapati on the hot pan without oil. Cook for about 30 seconds to 1 minute, until small bubbles begin to appear on the surface and the top looks dry.
  10. Flip and Oil: Flip the chapati over. Brush the first, partially cooked side lightly with oil or ghee.
  11. Cook and Press: Flip the chapati again so the oiled side is down. Brush the second side with oil. Gently press down on the edges with a spatula to ensure even cooking and encourage the chapati to puff up. Cook each side until golden-brown spots appear.
  12. Store and Serve: Remove the cooked chapati from the pan and place it in a serving dish lined with a clean kitchen towel or aluminum foil. Cover them completely to trap the steam and keep them soft and warm until all chapatis are cooked and ready to serve
